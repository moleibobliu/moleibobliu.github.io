Molei Liu

Welcome to my personal page!

I am now an assistant professor in Department of Biostatistics, Columbia University Mailman School of Public Health.

My research interests about statistics include high dimensional statistics, federated learning, semi-supervised learning, transfer learning, semiparametric method and theory, multiple testing, and model-X inference. Besides these modern statistics topics, I have also been working on electronic health record (EHR) and biobank data analysis.

I obtained my Ph.D. degree from Department of Biostatistics, Harvard Chan School of Public Health, advised by Tianxi Cai. My thesis advisor committee also includes Lucas Janson and Junwei Lu.

Prior to PhD, I did my undergraduate in the School of Math, Peking University and did my summar intern at Hongyu Zhao's group, Department of Biostatistics, Yale school of Public Health.
